The Beginning

In the summer of 2021, the global shipping crisis had reached the point at which it was almost completely unviable to import furniture frames from the far east. Since this was our main method of acquiring complicated frames, we faced a bit of a problem, but also an unlikely opportunity.

For the first time in many decades, there was an opportunity to bring some manufacturing back to the UK, as the financial element suddenly made sense in the face of the enormous shipping costs. It was this realisation (coupled with a vision and optimism regarding what this could bring to the business) that led to the beginning of our search for a suitable machine.

Many suitors presented themselves, claiming to be the machine manufacturers who could solve our problems, but most came up short - in one way or another. It was only after having heard out the big players in the game that a previously unknown to us company, Bacci, entered the fray. Their demonstrations were impressive, and the machine had one thing the others didnâ€™t: a sixth axis.

Once the paperwork was finished, we began the long wait for delivery. The day finally arrived in mid-July 2022. Three weeks of installation and training ensued and then we were left alone; the theory was there, but the specifics and knowledge needed for practicality still eluded us.

As is apt to happen, other projects then took the front seat. We were, however, provided some relief from our "frame-woes" by a deal struck with our big customer to purchase an enormous number at once. One thing after another meant that, by the end of 2022, we had still only cut theory-proving pieces on the machine.

Now, at the beginning of 2023, the 6-axis project is back on, and we are approaching it with a renewed vigour. I hope in the posts below to be able to bring you along on this journey, as it happens, to give a flavour of the difficulties we face in bringing our vision to fruition.

James Hearn - 2023
